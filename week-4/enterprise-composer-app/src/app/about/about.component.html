Ludwig van Beethoven


Photo via Good Free Photos
Ludwig van Beethoven was born in Germany around December 16, 1770, and died on March 26, 1827. Beethoven was a classical composer and was “widely regarded as the greatest composer who ever lived” (Britannica, n.d.). Beethoven was born into a musical family and was 'discovered' by composer Joseph Hayden when he was a teenager. Under Hayden's tutelage, Beethoven became a successful composer. His most famous compositions include Symphony No. 5 in C Minor, Symphony No. 7 in A Major, and Symphony No. 9 in D Minor (Britannica, n.d.). Beethoven experienced gradual hearing loss until he was completely deaf in 1819. He continued to compose music even when he couldn't hear, resulting in some of his most famous works.
